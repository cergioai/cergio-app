feat(results): tiered ranking per spec + marketplace confirm-availability spec

Per Tarik (2026-06-02): "the spec is that services get notified and
confirm ability to offer free services (which then has them show up
in the results). it's not like a search. All results need to have
'confirmed availability + confirmed ability to do service for free'
(or added a price to counter). Same the other way around (services
requesting free spotlights from Connectors). For testing, need to see
the services being notified, accepting, then their profiles showing
up. Same for Connectors.

The rank priority is:
  1 — highest match of friend recos + free (if free is requested)
  2 — if no free is requested, rank based on highest friend recos +
      within the budget price, then Connector recos + within budget
  Prioritize friend connections OVER budget for Cergio's pick — i.e.
    show friend-recommended service Out of Budget, AHEAD of a service
    that has NO friend connections.
  BUT: if a service has ≥1 friend reco + within budget, it ranks
    HIGHER than a service with more friend recos but over budget."

═══════════════ 1. Ranking — SHIPPED ═══════════════

`src/lib/rankResults.js`:
  Pure function. Six-tier classification, deterministic sort, zero
  dependencies. Easy to unit-test from a node REPL.

  Tier hierarchy (highest first):
    T1   friend_recos ≥ 1   AND  within_budget
    T2   friend_recos ≥ 1   AND  NOT within_budget
    T3   connector_recos ≥1 AND  within_budget
    T4   connector_recos ≥1 AND  NOT within_budget
    T5   no recos            AND  within_budget
    T6   everything else

  Within a tier:
    friend_count DESC > connector_count DESC > rating DESC
    > priceCents ASC > distance_miles ASC

  When `wantFree=true`:
    • Free services come BEFORE paid ones, full stop.
    • Within free, the same T1..T6 ordering applies.
    • Paid options follow as a fallback band so the page is never
      empty, but they sit below all free ones regardless of recos.

  First item gets the `pick=true` flag (Cergio's Pick badge).

  Exports:
    rankResults(services, { budgetCents, wantFree }) → ranked array
    applyPickFlag(ranked) → mutates pick=true on [0], false on rest
    classifyTier(service, opts) → integer tier 1..7 (exposed for tests)

  Verified against Tarik's worked example end-to-end:
    A: friend=1, $150, budget=$100  → T2 (over budget but has friend)
    B: friend=0, $80,  budget=$100  → T5 (within budget, no friend)
    C: friend=1, $90,  budget=$100  → T1 (friend AND within budget)
    Result: C > A > B  ✓

  Verified free-first behaviour:
    F1: free, friend=0, connector=1
    F2: free, friend=2, connector=0
    P1: $50,  friend=5, connector=0
    With wantFree=true: F2 > F1 > P1  ✓ (free wins despite P1 having
                                        more friend recos)

`src/screens/ResultsScreen.jsx`:
  Replaces the legacy two-line sort (just friend-bucket flag) with
  `applyPickFlag(rankResults(providersRaw, { budgetCents, wantFree }))`.
  Pulls `budgetCents` and `freeServices` from existing state — no
  wiring changes.
