══════════════════ (2) Earnings IA — running tape per transaction ═══

  Tarik: "the architecture of information isn't consistent in the
  earnings activity. eg: $63 earned that session (7% of 900 booking)
  but $250 was already earned in sessions before — so it should be
  +$0. The activity is a running tape of individual payments hitting
  your balance from individual transactions, with aggregates to
  pump up users."

  EarningsScreen.jsx — per-row effective credit:

    rowCapState now exposes:
      effectiveCents — the actual dollar amount that hit balance for
                       THIS transaction, after applying the per-friend
                       cap walked oldest→newest.
      rawCents       — the raw ledger amount (so the row caption can
                       still say "$X over the cap" when partial).

    Computation:
      before = sum of prior rows in same bucket (friend or chain path)
      room   = max(0, cap - before)
      eff    = min(this row's amount, room)

    Row display (right side):
      Before: "+$250" with a contradictory "$0 left ✓" pill
              (raw ledger amount even when cap was maxed earlier).
      After:  "+$0  CAP MAXED"  — when the friend's cap was already
                                  reached in a prior session.
      Or:     "+$187  PARTIAL · raw +$250" — when this row tipped
                                  the cap over and only $187 fit.
      Or:     "+$63"  — normal, full payout.

    Hero balance card:
      Was: sum of raw ledger amount_cents (could exceed real cap).
      Now: sum of effectiveCents per row + booking rows raw.
      So the user's headline balance matches what they could actually
      cash out — no more "$838 of $250 potential" feel.
