fix(parser): kill cross-domain fragment mislabels (SPEC-68 hardening)

Follow-up to the spine audit. Generic action words that span domains ("trim"
= hedge vs beard, "fix", "repair", "clean", "cut") were letting the substring
/ unigram / token-exact steps emit confident-WRONG matches — e.g.
"beard trim" → Landscaper, "fix my ac" → Handyman.

- AMBIGUOUS_TOKENS blocklist applied at the index build (unigram never indexes
  them) AND in every fallback scan (substring step 8 / normalized 8a / token
  step 9). A lone generic/short token can no longer decide a match; multi-word
  phrases (inherently specific) and provider-type/category terms still win.
- Net: high-confidence (≥0.60) emissions are correct; ambiguous phrases
  ("beard trim", "fix my ac") route to Claude instead of mislabeling.
- Verified: chef/plumber/cleaning/ES/PT bridges intact; "i need a chef who can
  cook ecuadorian food tonight" → Personal Chef. 74 qa flows pass.
